Why computer use — and reuse — matters

Benchmarks are converging

On OSWorld‑Verified, Gemini 3.5 Flash reports performance within fractions of a point of other frontier models — effectively a tie. As benchmarks converge, system design and economics matter more than raw scores.

Repeated work changes the cost curve

Agentic computer‑use workflows are token‑heavy. Gemini emphasizes low per‑token pricing and cached input discounts, while Super focuses on reusing prior computer actions directly through its computer‑use cache.

Security and realism

Once agents can operate logged‑in browsers, attackers adapt quickly. Coverage around Gemini highlights prompt‑injection and safety tradeoffs that every computer‑using agent must address deliberately.

Demand is growing

Consumer and enterprise interest in personal AI agents is accelerating across Asia‑Pacific and beyond, underscoring why durable designs matter.
